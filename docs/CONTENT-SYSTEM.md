# Content System — How to Turn Dayna's Brain Dumps into Brand-Aligned Copy
*Locked 2026-05-31. From "Case Study Format" + "Dayna Content Transformation System." This is the process; `BRAND-VOICE.md` is the standard the process is judged against.*

## Core principle
The goal is **NOT** to mimic Dayna's exact phrasing, cadence, or vocabulary. The goal is to: understand how Dayna interprets human situations, identify the emotional and operational realities underneath a topic, and communicate them with grounded clarity, observational intelligence, and practical humanity.

This is **not a keyword-based voice system. It is a reasoning-based communication framework.** Prioritize perception, implication, emotional calibration, audience psychology, practical clarity, and trust — above stylistic mimicry.

**The AI's job is reconstruction**, never: lightly editing a brain dump, preserving stream-of-consciousness structure, or just "cleaning up" wording.

## The two-layer client model (always know which one you're writing for)
**Layer 1 — Practical efficiency clients:** busy professionals, apartment setups, move support, resale pickup, IKEA assembly, tech setup, operational outsourcing, convenience.
Emotional state: *"I do not have time or bandwidth for this."* — NOT *"My life is falling apart."*
Tone: ease, speed, relief, trust, efficiency, follow-through, reduced friction. **Do not inject emotional heaviness.**

**Layer 2 — Emotional / transitional clients:** grief, aging parents, estate transitions, household overwhelm, long-term storage, emotionally loaded homes.
Tone: steadiness, calm, dignity, emotional pacing, trust, operational clarity — but still grounded and practical.
Never: therapy-coded, inspirational, grief-aesthetic, emotionally indulgent, spiritually branded.

## The process (run these in order)

**Stage 1 — Raw brain dump.** Treat Dayna's input as cognitive source material, not final messaging. It will contain repetition, tangents, unfinished/contradictory thoughts, shorthand, processing language, emotional charge. Expected.

**Stage 2 — Intent extraction.** What is the actual point? What emotional truth is underneath? What tension/problem is observed? What perception should the audience leave with? What relief/value is offered? Is it practical / emotional / convenience / trust / efficiency / identity / operational / transitional? Summarize the core objective in plain language.

**Stage 3 — Signal vs static.** SIGNAL = valuable observations, truths, tensions, specificity, emotional insight, positioning. STATIC = repetition, processing loops, emotional over-explaining, accidental harshness, verbal clutter, unnecessary philosophy, vague abstraction. Preserve observations/insights/emotional truths/practical realities/reasoning; discard raw processing/redundancy/spiraling/emotional overexposure.

**Stage 4 — Emotional weight calibration.** Identify the TRUE weight and don't exceed it. Examples: fast resale pickup = low (convenience, time relief); post-move unpacking = moderate (cognitive relief, settling); estate transition = high (pacing, dignity, memory, family dynamics); busy professional setup = moderate (efficiency, trust, bandwidth). **Do not assign emotional depth indiscriminately.**

**Stage 5 — Brand risk detection.** Flag drift into: therapy language, luxury branding, startup jargon, abstract philosophy, emotional performance, excessive sentimentality, self-importance, "founder manifesto" tone, corporate language, over-polished writing, quirky branding voice. Name the wording that triggered concern and why.

**Stage 6 — Structural rebuild (concept first).** Rebuild around: grounded observations, practical clarity, audience recognition, emotional realism, subtle trust-building, natural cadence, operational competence. Final copy should feel distilled, intentional, observant, capable, natural — never polished-to-death, over-written, luxury-branded, emotionally narrated, or startup-poetic.

**Stage 7 — Brand guardrail audit.** Before finalizing, ask: does this accidentally sound preachy? luxury-coded? emotionally manipulative? too corporate? self-important? too polished to trust? therapy-adjacent? spiritually vague? condescending? overly sentimental? generic lifestyle branding? If yes → rewrite.

**Stage 8 — Final output + line-by-line notes.** Copy should feel human, observant, useful, emotionally intelligent, naturally confident, grounded, commercially clear. Audience feels understood and relieved — not marketed to, therapized, or manipulated. For each major rewrite, show: ORIGINAL LINE → ISSUE DETECTED → TRANSFORMATION LOGIC → FINAL VERSION (focus on perception, implication, emotional calibration, specificity, clarity, audience psychology, tone — not grammar).

## Worked example (the target quality)
- ORIGINAL: "I provide emotionally intelligent continuity support for evolving household ecosystems."
- ISSUE: Abstract. Over-branded. Emotionally vague. Corporate-luxury. No real human image.
- LOGIC: The underlying idea is people need practical support during life transitions and household complexity. The rewrite should create immediate recognition, sound human, ground the concept in lived experience.
- FINAL: **"The move happened months ago. Half the house still feels temporary."**

## Final rule
Optimize for **recognition over performance.** Emotional intelligence is IMPLIED through observation, never EXPLAINED through philosophy. The strongest writing makes people think *"Yes. Exactly."*
