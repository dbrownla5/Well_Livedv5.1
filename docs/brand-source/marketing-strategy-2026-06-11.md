# The Well Lived Citizen — Marketing & Conversion Strategy
*Distilled 2026-06-11 from Dayna's marketing/copy working sessions. This is the "marketing skeleton" layer — page structure, customer language, and what justifies the price. Pair with BRAND-VOICE.md (the sound) and SERVICES-PRICING.md (the detail).*

## The one principle: sell the OUTCOME, not the labor
$495 does NOT buy "closet organization." The moment you name rooms/bins/hours, the customer does the math ("$124/hr?") and compares you to TaskRabbit, organizers, cleaners, college students — a losing comparison. $495 buys *the thing that's been hanging over them for months finally getting handled by someone competent.* The service is the mechanism; **the relief is the product.**

What justifies the price (all outcomes):
1. **Speed** — "this stops eating my bandwidth by the end of the week."
2. **Decision relief** — "someone competent is making the decisions *with* me." (Indecision is expensive — the storage unit proves it.)
3. **Momentum** — "the thing that's followed me around for six months finally moves."
4. **Personalization** — a *fractional competent adult*: they don't re-explain everything, coordinate four vendors, make twenty decisions alone, or become a project manager.

## The ladder — it's a business with levels, not one sentence (two customer paths)
- **Level 1 — Easy money (homepage conversion).** Burnt-out millennials: "take the bags, sell the stuff, I don't want to learn Poshmark, I just want it gone." Low-trust, fast decision, income NOW. **Resale leads.** Pitch: *"Fill the bag. I handle the rest."*
- **Level 2 — Practical help.** The Reset, House Calls, Move Wrap-Up. Pitch: *"The thing you've been meaning to handle finally gets handled."*
- **Level 3 — The trust business (deeper / referral).** Gayle's-daughter-in-Florida: "Mom's lived there 30 years, I can't get there every month, I need someone I trust." One intro becomes years of work — Legacy, parent support, downsizing, Closeout. The referral is literally *"Call Dayna. She'll know what to do."*

**The homepage sells *easy*. The site earns *safe*.** One paragraph can't do both — stop trying.

## Customer language (Susan), NOT founder language
Susan never says "operational overwhelm / continuity / stewardship / transition management." She says:
- "I don't even know where to start." · "Every time I look at it I get tired." · "I can do it, I just haven't." · "It's become this whole thing." · "It's hanging over my head." · "It's not hard, it's just a lot."
- **Storage unit:** "I'm tired of paying for that damn storage unit." · "I don't even know what's in there anymore."
- **Closet:** "There's probably thousands of dollars in that closet." · "I don't have the energy to deal with it."
- **Move:** "The move happened, we just never finished." · "Half the house still lives in boxes."
- **Parent:** "I just need someone to check on my mom." · "Dad says he's fine. He is not fine."
- **Garage:** "I can't park in my garage." · "It's become a dumping ground."

**Words OUT of public copy:** operational overwhelm, continuity, stewardship, transition management, optimize, elevated.

## What each service actually sells (the "customer hears")
- **Resale** = *done-for-you decisions.* "You fill the bag. I figure out the rest." The bags leave, money shows up. Strongest, most tangible offer + income now.
- **Move Wrap-Up / Closeout** = *trusted proxy.* "Leave. I'll finish the last 20%." The clearest outcome-sell in the business.
- **House Calls** = "someone helping me keep life running." Problems caught while still small.
- **Legacy Inventory** = *clarity before urgency.* Fewer surprises when decisions eventually have to be made.
- **The Reset** = *cognitive relief.* "The thing hanging over me, handled by tonight."

The thread under all of it: **clarity before urgency** / *"someone competent is finally helping me carry this."*
