Yes, there are several professional roles and business models that manage household tasks for multiple clients without being full-time personal assistants. These services range from specialized property management to high-level strategic oversight.
## Fractional and Multi-Client Management

* Fractional Estate Managers: These professionals provide high-level management expertise to several clients on a part-time or project-specific basis. They act like consultants for households that do not need daily oversight but have seasonal or complex needs, such as coordinating renovations or preparing properties for arrival.
* Personal Concierge Services: Unlike personal assistants who typically work for one principal, personal concierges often manage multiple clients simultaneously. They provide flexibility, working on an hourly or as-needed basis to handle varied tasks from booking luxury travel to coordinating unique personal experiences.
* Home Concierge Companies: These businesses offer property-focused support for multiple homeowners. Their services include regular maintenance (landscaping, pool care), security monitoring, and vendor management for repairs or deliveries. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6] 

## Strategic vs. Operational Roles

* Household Managers: Often described as the "COO of the home," they oversee the day-to-day operations and logistics of a household. While they might work for one large estate, many serve families with multiple properties, ensuring seamless management across different locations.
* Estate Managers: These individuals focus on high-level management of large properties or multiple estates. Their role is strategic, involving budget management, event coordination, and the supervision of other staff like housekeepers or private chefs.
* Lifestyle Managers: This is a hybrid role that coordinates complex schedules, household logistics, and high-touch service needs across multiple residences. They are often used by high-net-worth individuals who travel frequently and require a dynamic coordinator to anticipate needs globally. [1, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11] 

## Specialized Service Providers

* Personal Chefs: While a private chef works for one household, a personal chef typically serves multiple clients. They may specialize in weekly meal prep, where they visit multiple homes to stock fridges with ready-to-eat dishes tailored to specific dietary needs.
* Executive Housekeepers: More than a standard cleaner, they often take on managerial duties such as supervising outside vendors, managing household inventory, and setting systematic cleaning schedules for a family.
* Virtual Lifestyle Managers: These assistants perform tasks remotely for various clients. They function as fractional service providers, handling everything from sourcing babysitters to booking transportation as independent contractors. [12, 13, 14]
